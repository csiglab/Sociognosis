01 — Objective

Constructing the DESA

The goal of Sociognosis is to stabilize a generative system that produces a Domain Epistemic Artifact Set (DESA) representing social reality. The DESA renders social phenomena intelligible as a structured domain of analysis and action—connecting abstract epistemic categories to real-world decision-making.

Rather than describing sovereign states directly, the methodology decomposes social reality into distinct analytical spaces and encodes them through a formal ontic–epistemic schema. Each artifact in the DESA is a node in this schema, typed by category and linked by typed relationships.

Cognoscere ad agendum. — To know in order to act. The pipeline is designed so that every artifact is, in principle, connected to a purpose: explanation, prediction, control, or intervention.

02 — Epistemic Pipeline

From Reality to Artifact

The generative system that produces the DESA follows a layered pipeline. Each stage is itself an epistemic category in the schema. The pipeline stabilizes how raw reality becomes structured, queryable knowledge.

Reality Section

A bounded segment of social reality targeted by epistemic practice—observable phenomena, latent mechanisms, causal architectures, and state trajectories across time.

Observation Interface

The sensorimotor boundary that transduces reality into a persistent encoded artifact—datasets, records, laws, and signals that become raw material for inference.

Representation Form

The encoding format or substrate in which artifacts are expressed—DAGs, structural equations, matrices, graphs, or logical formulas chosen for expressiveness.

Concrete Artifact

A structured object encoding claims over reality—a fitted model, dataset, equation, or graph. Each artifact is the output of applying an operator to prior artifacts.

03 — Analytical Spaces

Sub-Project Decomposition

Sociognosis decomposes social reality into distinct analytical spaces. Each space is a sub-project that renders a specific dimension of the social world intelligible and comparable.

Index

  • JSON-backed data model

Product Space

Analysis of economic products and their relationships within the global production network. Maps industrial complexity, export capabilities, and productive knowledge via a technique → product matrix.

  • Technique → Product Matrix
  • Capability mapping

Firm Space

Mapping organizational structures and corporate entities within economic systems. Includes modeling templates for firm types, financial structures, and industrial sections.

Literary Space

Exploration of literary works as social artifacts and their role in shaping cultural reality. Treats texts as epistemic objects encoding social knowledge and narrative structures.

  • Text-as-artifact analysis
  • Cultural representation mapping
  • Narrative structure indexing

04 — Ontic–Epistemic Schema

Categories of Epistemic Elements

Every node in the DESA belongs to one of the formal categories defined in the JSON Schema (data/idx/schema/schema.json). Categories are organized into functional groups reflecting the ontic–epistemic structure of the pipeline.

Group Category Role in the DESA
Foundational Reality The ontological substrate—actual states, structures, processes, entities, and causal dynamics of social reality.
Foundational Epistemic Domain The bounded segment of reality targeted and modeled by epistemic practice.
Foundational Epistemic Gap Portion of reality for which no adequate artifact or explanatory structure yet exists.
Observation Observation Interface The boundary that transduces reality into a persistent encoded artifact.
Observation Observation Encoding The formal standard for encoded observational data.
Observation Encoding Substrate The medium in which information is physically instantiated (paper, signal, etc.).
Representation Epistemic Representation Form The encoding format or substrate in which artifacts are expressed (DAG, equation, graph, matrix).
Representation Concrete Epistemic Artifact A structured object encoding claims over reality—models, datasets, equations, simulations.
Agency Epistemic Agent Entities that perform epistemic operations—scientists, institutions, ML systems.
Agency Epistemic Act Primitive operations: attending, discriminating, remembering, intervening.
Agency Epistemic Process Ordered sequences of tool applications over time—the dynamics of knowledge construction.
Teleology Epistemic Purpose The telos motivating inquiry—explanation, prediction, control, discovery.
Teleology Epistemic Goal Specific desired epistemic outcomes guiding inquiry and validation.
Normative Epistemic Standard Normative criteria for evaluating validity—consistency, significance, reproducibility.
Normative Epistemic Constraint Limitations bounding what can be known—noise, complexity, identifiability.
Infrastructure Epistemic Infrastructure Supporting environment—sensors, laboratories, databases, publications.
Infrastructure Epistemic Feedback Signals from reality that confirm or resist prior predictions—drivers of learning.
Methodological Epistemic Principle Foundational rules governing inquiry—empiricism, falsifiability, parsimony.
Methodological Epistemic Strategy Adaptive planning logic for resource allocation and inquiry sequencing.
Methodological Epistemic Framework Conceptual systems structuring interpretation—logic, causal inference, game theory.
Methodological Epistemic Operator Formal mechanisms for constructing, transforming, or validating artifacts.

05 — Guiding Principles

Inference & Data

The generative system is governed by a small set of methodological commitments that shape how artifacts are produced, validated, and connected.

Inference-Powered Forms

Prefer representation forms that can be powered by large-scale data inference—taking raw data such as laws, regulations, or records, and inferring structured metrics from them.

Refinement Loop

The generative system is itself the subject of continuous study, refinement, and improvement—with emphasis on consistency, expressiveness, and explanatory power.

Action Coupling

Artifacts are systematically linked to practical activity systems, connecting the DESA to real-world actions, interventions, and decision-making processes.

Traceable Provenance

Each artifact carries metadata, confidence scores, source references, and an audit trail—making the DESA auditable and reproducible.
