Introduction

This project is organized around the idea of mapping the product space and the underlying production processes. These processes are, in turn, enabled by techniques — including core production techniques as well as adjacent techniques such as regulatory compliance, legal structuring, tax optimization, logistics, financing, and other supporting capabilities.

Production is one of the core activities of human society, providing the goods and services required for survival, prosperity, and development. By formally representing these structures, we gain the capacity to analyse industrial development, identify capability gaps, and understand the recursive complexity inherent in modern production.

The core objectives of this project are supported by concepts and frameworks developed in Arbitriologia, Produceologia, Social Ontology, and related fields — disciplines concerned with the formal study of decision, production, and social reality.

"To understand what a society can produce, one must first understand what it is capable of producing — and that requires mapping the space of possibilities."

Approach

Methodology

The analytical foundations underlying this project

Graph-Based Modeling

Nodes represent entities; edges represent structured relationships. This enables topological analysis of dependency structures and capability networks.

Hierarchical Decomposition

Production systems are decomposed from final products down to fundamental techniques, revealing the layered structure of productive knowledge.

Relationship Taxonomy

Relationships are classified into families — value chain, functional, capability dependency, structural, governance, and market — enabling systematic analysis.

Historical Contextualization

Each entity is situated within its historical trajectory, tracing the chronology of emergence, adoption, and evolution of production capabilities.

Process Characterization

Production processes are modeled through their inputs, outputs, transformation logic, and constraints — providing an operational view of production.

Capability Analysis

The accumulation, transfer, and combination of techniques is studied to understand how complex production becomes possible over time.
